Simulab

Study Data Generator

Design a simple study and generate your own dataset to analyze. Supports between-subjects, repeated-measures, and mixed designs. Datasets are generated on a server and are unique to you — this page never has access to the true effect sizes behind your data.

1Study design

Are you comparing different people (between-subjects), the same people over time (repeated measures), or both at once (mixed)?

2Your grouping variable (independent variable)

Think of the conditions or categories you're comparing — two teaching methods, two dosage levels, present vs. absent. Keep group labels short and make sure every participant fits into exactly one group.

This tool currently supports one two-level grouping variable and one measured variable.

3Repeated-measures structure

Name your two time points (e.g., Before/After, Pretest/Posttest), then estimate how consistent people tend to be relative to each other across that gap — that consistency is what makes a repeated-measures test more powerful than comparing two independent groups.

4Your measured variable (dependent variable)

Base the range on the instrument itself (a 0–100 quiz, a 1–7 Likert scale); set the mean near the middle of that range unless you have a specific reason to expect otherwise. As a starting estimate, SD is often about a sixth of the full range — check a pilot study or published norms if you have one.

5Predicted relationships & sample size

Base your expected effect(s) on prior research or theory — weak effects are the norm in psychology, while strong ones usually only show up under tightly controlled manipulations. Adjust the sample size and watch the power estimates update to see how the two trade off.

6Generate your dataset

Your name/ID just needs to be unique to you and something you'll use consistently — a pseudonym is fine, as long as it's yours all semester (in case your instructor ever needs to look up your submission). Ask your instructor for the class password if you don't have it.

Doesn't have to be your real identity — just unique to you.
